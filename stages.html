Life Stages

Dachshunds have different needs as they grow. A puppy, adult, and senior dachshund all need care, but the focus changes at each stage of life.

Stage Care Chart

Stage Average Age Immunizations / Checkups Diet Exercise
Puppy 0–1 year Puppy vaccines, deworming, and regular vet visits Puppy food with measured meals Short walks and gentle play
Adult 1–7 years Yearly checkups and updated vaccines Adult dog food with portion control Daily walks and regular playtime
Senior 7+ years More frequent vet visits and joint/back checks Senior dog food if recommended by a vet Gentle walks and low-impact activity

Puppy Stage

Puppy dachshunds are curious, energetic, and still learning how to behave. This is the best time to begin basic training, socialization, and safe habits.

  • Teach simple commands
  • Start potty training
  • Introduce them to new people and places
  • Prevent jumping from furniture early

Adult Stage

Adult dachshunds usually settle into their personality and daily routine. They still need exercise, mental stimulation, and careful weight management.

  • Keep a consistent feeding schedule
  • Give daily walks and playtime
  • Watch their weight
  • Continue training and socialization

Senior Stage

Senior dachshunds may slow down and need extra comfort. They can still live happy lives, but owners should pay closer attention to mobility and health.

  • Use ramps or steps more often
  • Provide a soft, comfortable bed
  • Schedule regular vet checkups
  • Watch for pain, stiffness, or trouble walking
