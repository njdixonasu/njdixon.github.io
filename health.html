Health Tips

Ear Care

Check and clean your dachshund’s ears regularly to prevent infections. A quick wipe once a week helps keep them healthy.

Bathing

Dachshunds don’t need frequent baths. Once every 1–2 months is usually enough, unless they get especially dirty.

Teeth Cleaning

Brushing your dog’s teeth a few times a week helps prevent plaque buildup and keeps their breath fresh. Dog-safe toothpaste is recommended.

Dachshund Health

Dachshunds are fun, loyal, and full of personality, but they require special care because of their long backs and short legs.

Back Care

Dachshunds are especially prone to back problems because of their long spine and short legs. This body shape puts extra stress on their back, which can lead to injuries like slipped discs or long-term pain if not properly cared for.

How to help prevent back problems:

  • Avoid letting your dachshund jump on and off furniture
  • Use ramps or steps for beds and couches
  • Support their chest and back when picking them up
  • Limit rough play that involves twisting or jumping
  • Keep them at a healthy weight

Weight Control

Maintaining a healthy weight is one of the most important parts of keeping a dachshund healthy. Extra weight puts additional pressure on their spine, increasing the risk of back injuries and joint problems.

Typical weight ranges:

  • Miniature dachshund: up to 11 pounds
  • Standard dachshund: 16–32 pounds

Keeping your dachshund within a healthy range helps protect their back and joints over time.

Feeding proper portions, limiting treats, and making sure your dachshund gets regular exercise are key to maintaining a healthy weight.

Regular Checkups

Routine vet visits help catch any problems early and keep your dog healthy throughout their life.
