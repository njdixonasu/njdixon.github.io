Diet Tips

Measure Food

Always measure your dog’s food to avoid overfeeding.

Limit Treats

Treats should only make up a small part of their diet.

Fresh Water

Make sure your dachshund always has access to clean water.

Diet

A proper diet is essential for keeping your dachshund healthy and maintaining a safe weight. Because of their long backs, even small amounts of extra weight can lead to serious health problems.

What to Feed

High-quality dog food that is appropriate for your dachshund’s age and size is the best option. Look for foods with real meat as the main ingredient and avoid fillers when possible.

  • Dry kibble for balanced nutrition
  • Wet food for added moisture (in moderation)
  • Occasional healthy treats

Portion Control

Dachshunds are prone to overeating, so portion control is very important. Feeding too much can quickly lead to weight gain and put stress on their back.

Most dachshunds should be fed twice a day with measured portions. Always follow feeding guidelines based on your dog’s weight and activity level.

Foods to Avoid

Never feed your dachshund these foods:

  • Chocolate
  • Grapes and raisins
  • Onions and garlic
  • Cooked bones
  • Excess fatty or salty foods

These foods can be dangerous or even toxic to dogs, so it’s important to keep them out of reach.
